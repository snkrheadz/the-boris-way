# Craft: Academic (scholarly writing, literature reviews, citations, grants, peer review)

What excellent scholarly work IS, checkable. research.md governs evidence (triangulation,
disconfirming, confidence labels); this file adds the conventions of academic work.

## 1. Citations (the integrity layer)

- Every non-original claim cites the source you actually consulted. Citing a paper you have
  not read, via someone else's summary, is citation laundering; cite the summary or read the
  paper. An abstract supports only what the abstract says: claims resting on methods, results
  tables, or limitations require reading those sections (or writing "per abstract").
- VERIFY EVERY REFERENCE, two checks, both actually performed, never from memory: (a)
  existence: authors, year, title, venue, DOI resolve together as ONE record (the classic AI
  failure is a plausible mashup: real authors, real venue, wrong title or year); (b) support:
  the source backs the exact sentence citing it, in direction and magnitude; locate the
  passage or move the claim. A fabricated, garbled, or non-supporting citation is
  credibility-ending; existence alone does not clear it.
- Check status where the DOI resolves: retracted papers are cited only to discuss the
  retraction; preprints are labeled as preprints, never laundered into settled findings.
- Quote exactly with page numbers, or paraphrase honestly: the test is whether the source's
  author would accept your sentence with its qualifiers intact (a hedged finding paraphrased
  as definitive is misquotation). Primary sources over reviews for load-bearing claims.
- Citation density honest: you can state in one clause what each citation contributes; if
  you cannot, cut it. Cite what shaped the work, not a wall of tangential padding.

## 2. Literature reviews

- Method first: define the search strategy, databases, and inclusion criteria before reading;
  report the funnel with counts (hits per query, screened, included, excluded and why) so
  the selection is auditable. An unsystematic review is a vibes tour.
- Organize by theme and argument, never paper-by-paper ("Smith found X. Jones found Y." is an
  annotated bibliography, not a review).
- Map agreements, disagreements, and the methods differences that explain the disagreements;
  steelman the findings that cut against your position to the standard of research.md
  section 2 (their proponents would sign your summary of them).
- End at the gap: the review exists to show precisely what is unknown and why your question
  follows.

## 3. Structure (IMRaD and its cousins)

- Abstract states, in order: problem, method, key finding (with the number), implication.
  An abstract that teases without stating the finding wastes the reader.
- Introduction is a funnel: field, the specific gap, this work, contributions as an explicit
  list.
- Methods reproducible by a stranger: versions, parameters, data provenance, exclusions and
  their reasons. If a peer could not rerun it, it is not methods, it is marketing.
- Results report findings without interpretation; discussion interprets, compares to prior
  work, and states limitations BEFORE reviewers do (naming your own limitations buys
  credibility; hiding them spends it). Scale claims to the sample: one study of n=40
  undergraduates "suggests X in this population"; it does not establish X.
- Effect sizes and uncertainty, not just p-values (data.md section 2 applies in full);
  exploratory vs confirmatory analysis labeled as such.
- Internal consistency: every number appearing in two places (abstract, text, table, figure)
  is identical; totals sum; N stays constant or each change is explained. One term per
  concept throughout: switching synonyms mid-paper reads as two concepts.

## 4. Grants and proposals

- The aims page carries the award: problem, why now, why you (evidence of capability), the
  approach, expected outcomes. Most reviewers mostly read this page.
- Aims independent: if aim 1 fails, aims 2 and 3 must still stand (interdependent aims are a
  single point of failure reviewers punish).
- Answer the call: address each of the funder's stated review criteria explicitly, in the
  funder's vocabulary, so a reviewer can fill the scoresheet from your text.
- Write for the skimming reviewer: first sentence of every paragraph carries the argument;
  figures that explain themselves; the innovation stated plainly, once, without inflation.
- Budget lines trace to activities; feasibility shown with preliminary results or a pilot.

## 5. Peer review

- Giving: open with a faithful summary (proves the paper was read); judge substance before
  presentation: do the data and analysis support each claim, are controls and baselines
  adequate, does the sample license the generalization; separate major issues
  (validity-threatening) from minor (presentation); every criticism actionable ("add a
  control for X" not "unconvincing"); colleague tone, not judge.
- Receiving: respond point by point, quote the exact change made and where; disagree with
  evidence and civility; thank reviewers for catches that saved you.

## 6. Integrity

- No plagiarism, including self-plagiarism; run the check before submission, not after.
- AI assistance disclosed per the venue's policy; authorship reflects real intellectual
  contribution (gift and ghost authorship are both violations).
- Data and code availability stated; preregistration where the field supports it.

## 7. Ban list

- Unverified or invented references; "it is well known that" without a citation.
- "Numerous studies show" resting on one or two citations; generalizing a single study
  (state its n and population next to the claim instead).
- Citing a paper's speculation, or its abstract's spin, as if it were the paper's finding.
- Overclaiming verbs ("proves", "establishes definitively") where "suggests" or "is
  consistent with" is the truth.
- Hiding limitations; burying contrary results in supplements.
- Novelty theater (renaming an existing idea); salami-slicing one study into five papers.
- Passive-voice fog everywhere (prefer active where the venue allows); abstracts that
  withhold the finding.
- Citation stuffing to flatter likely reviewers.

## 8. Verification checklist (every pass)

1. Reference audit: EVERY citation (a) resolves as one real record (authors, year, venue,
   DOI) and (b) supports the exact sentence it anchors; retraction and preprint status
   noted. Output the audit list.
2. Review organized by theme, funnel counts reported, explicit gap statement?
3. Methods rerun-able by a stranger (versions, data, parameters, exclusions)?
4. Effect sizes and uncertainty reported; exploratory labeled; claims scaled to the sample?
5. Numbers identical across abstract, text, tables, figures; one term per concept?
6. Limitations section present and honest before submission?
7. Abstract: problem, method, finding with number, implication, in order?
8. For grants: aims independent, review criteria answered, first-sentence skim test passed?
